# CLAUDE.md — Working rules for this repository

This is a personal AI engineering learning repository. Its job is to preserve what I learn and turn it into durable, reusable capability. Follow these rules when helping maintain it.

## Maintenance rules

- **Keep [LEARNING_HUB.md](LEARNING_HUB.md) current.** It is the front door. When focus, active resources, or status change, update it.
- **Put raw lesson notes in the relevant course folder** under `courses/`. Routine per-part notes are appended to that course's `MODULE_LOG.md`, preferably through `/capture-lesson`. Use [templates/lesson-note.md](templates/lesson-note.md) for a standalone or deeper lesson note when more detail is useful. A separate file per module is not required.
- **Promote reusable lessons into playbooks.** When a note contains a technique I'll reuse, distill it into `playbooks/` rather than leaving it buried in course notes.
- **Add important terminology to [GLOSSARY.md](GLOSSARY.md)** as it comes up.
- **Update [SKILLS_MATRIX.md](SKILLS_MATRIX.md) only when evidence supports progress.** A score change needs a concrete evidence statement. Do not raise a score based only on watching or discussing material.
- **Add completed projects to [PORTFOLIO.md](PORTFOLIO.md)** once they're actually done, with links to the work.

## Writing style

- Keep writing concise, specific, and easy to review.
- Prefer direct sentences and concrete detail over filler.
- Include exact commands where they help.

## Accuracy rules (important)

- **Clearly distinguish completed work, planned work, and pending backfill.** Use consistent labels so status is never ambiguous.
- **Never invent completed work.** Do not claim something was verified, dated, linked, or finished when it was not.
- **Do not fabricate module content.** For courses with pending backfill, seed only what is explicitly verified and mark the rest as pending.
- **Exclude confidential or sensitive work information.** No proprietary details, credentials, internal names, or anything that shouldn't be public.

## How to engage

- **Challenge proposed plans when they create unnecessary risk or complexity.** Explain the tradeoffs and recommend a better approach instead of agreeing automatically.
- When unsure whether something counts as verified, ask rather than assume.

## Status vocabulary

Use these labels consistently across files:

- **Done** — completed and verified, with evidence.
- **In progress** — actively being worked.
- **Planned** — intended, not started.
- **Backfill pending** — happened, but not yet documented in detail here.
